What is Bikram Sambat?

Bikram Sambat (BS), also written Vikram Samvat, is Nepal's official calendar. It runs roughly 56 years and 8 months ahead of the Gregorian (AD) calendar, and the Nepali New Year falls in mid-April rather than January 1. Unlike AD months, which always have 28 to 31 days, BS months vary between 29 and 32 days and the exact count changes from year to year. That's why a quick mental formula doesn't work reliably — this tool uses a verified day-count table for every BS year instead of an estimate.

Common uses for date conversion

Citizenship & passport forms Convert your BS date of birth to AD for passport applications, visas, and the DV lottery, where only the Gregorian date is accepted.
School & job applications Academic certificates and admission forms in Nepal use BS, while foreign universities and employers usually expect AD.
Bank & legal documents Land records (lalpurja), bank account forms, and contracts often carry BS dates that need an AD equivalent for cross-border use.
Birthdays & anniversaries Quickly check what your Nepali birthday or anniversary falls on this year in the English calendar, or vice versa.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert BS to AD?

Select "Nepali → English (BS to AD)" above, choose the Bikram Sambat year, month, and day, then press Convert date. The Gregorian date, weekday, and Devanagari date all appear instantly.

How do I convert AD to BS?

Select "English → Nepali (AD to BS)", choose the Gregorian year, month, and day, then press Convert date. The Bikram Sambat date appears in both English and Devanagari script.

What date range does this converter support?

BS 1970 to BS 2090, which covers approximately AD 1913 to AD 2034. This range covers virtually every birth date, document date, and near-future planning date you're likely to need.

How accurate is this converter?

It uses a verified lookup table of the exact number of days in every BS month for every supported year, rather than an averaged formula. It has been tested for round-trip accuracy (BS → AD → BS) across the full supported range.
